My Story

How I became a technical writer.

United States

I grew up in the United States, and I graduated from Lewis & Clark College in Portland, Oregon. I loved researching, editing, interviewing, and writing. One of my campus jobs was to be an English tutor for foreign exchange students. I regularly reviewed texts written by non-native English speakers.

Ecuador

After I graduated college, I moved to Ecuador after college to work as a language arts teacher at the Center for Inter-American Studies in Cuenca. I taught English writing and composition courses to Ecuadorian high school students. I taught my students how to write for a target audience, and how to make their writing more concise. I also initiated publishing projects at the Amaru Wildlife Park. I built relationships with a local newspaper, wrote six chapters (___ words) of a book documenting the park’s conservation efforts, and edited technical documents, including a veterinary care manual.

United States

In March 2020, I was awarded a Fulbright grant, but the COVID-19 pandemic delayed the start of my grant. I moved back to Seattle to work as a contact tracer for the COVID response effort. I was given an interview script in English and Spanish and conducted 10 interviews per day, and documented quantitative and qualitative data for epidemiologists. I realized that rephrasing small prompts and phrases changed responses. I would alter phrasing to preserve epidemiologist's intended meaning and convey complex concepts as quickly and succinctly as possible.

Colombia

The US State Department allowed Fulbright grantees to return to Colombia in 2021. I was placed at the University of Antonio Nariño in Bogotá, where I created courses on medical English, US arts and literature, and English grammar. One of my main responsibilities was to edit academic research conducted by university professors.

I was promoted to Senior Fulbright Grantee. I was a staff mentor for five first-time grantees. I moved to the Universidad Tecnológica y Pedagogica de Colombia. I was given a small budget to manage for teacher-training projects.
